# Tier 6 Kickoff Gate Brief

## Purpose

Define a constrained Tier 6 start that preserves closure stability while introducing geopolitics mechanisms with clear, testable loop effects.

## Scope For Slice 1

- In scope: Elections, sanctions.
- Out of scope: Full wars, civil conflict, occupation, treaty debt reconstruction mechanics.

## Slice 1 Objectives

- Introduce election cycles in democratic blocs with policy stance shifts.
- Introduce sanctions as targeted trade/finance constraints with reroute pressure.
- Preserve explainability fields so observed effects can be traced.

## Upward Feedback Loops (Micro to Macro)

1. Household stress and unemployment raise populism pressure; populism pressure changes election outcomes and policy stance.
2. Firm distress and supply stress increase bloc-level intervention pressure, feeding policy shifts that alter macro labor and credit conditions.
3. Trade friction at firm level accumulates into bloc trade-shock evidence and FX pressure.

## Downward Feedback Loops (Macro to Micro)

1. Post-election policy stance changes alter labor demand bias, credit cost bias, and migration pressure; firms and households react in next cycles.
2. Sanctions raise import/supply friction and financing constraints, reducing firm capacity and household purchasing power.
3. Bloc-level geopolitical pressure changes risk and confidence channels for hiring, expansion, and savings behavior.

## Entry Criteria

- Current closure baseline and latest run artifacts are available.
- Tier 6 observability fields are defined before mechanics land.
- Sanctions/elections are feature-flagged or isolated by phase guard.

## Exit Criteria

- Elections and sanctions run in simulation without breaking save compatibility.
- New evidence fields visible in inspector/telemetry outputs.
- Closure regression command remains runnable and results are documented.

## Non-Goals

- No war engine in Slice 1.
- No conflict casualty/refugee modeling in Slice 1.
- No treaty reconstruction and reparations in Slice 1.
